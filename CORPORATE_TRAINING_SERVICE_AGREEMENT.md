## 6. LEARNING OUTCOMES

Upon completion, participants will:

✅ **Electrical Safety**
- Apply NFPA 70E and OSHA safety procedures
- Demonstrate hazard recognition and mitigation
- Execute lockout/tagout procedures correctly

✅ **DC/AC Fundamentals**
- Apply Ohm's Law and Watt's Law to problems
- Design and troubleshoot simple circuits
- Calculate power, voltage, current accurately

✅ **Code Compliance**
- Interpret NEC code articles for safe installation
- Select correct conductor sizes and protection
- Design compliant grounding systems

✅ **Hands-On Skills**
- Perform code-compliant wire splicing and terminations
- Install switches, receptacles, and GFCI/AFCI protection
- Bend conduit with accuracy
- Build and test complete circuits

✅ **Competencies**
- [Module 1 competency: Safety, tooling, DC circuits, AC circuits, splicing, switches, subpanels, conduit, grounding, solar design, battery systems, commissioning]
- Participants earn credentials that are portable and industry-recognized

## 8. ASSESSMENT & CERTIFICATION

**Grading:**
- Module quizzes: 40% of grade
- Hands-on competency demonstrations: 40% of grade
- Final capstone project: 20% of grade
- Passing score: 70% overall

**Certificates:**
- Participants scoring 70%+ receive WAI Electrical Apprenticeship Certificate
- Digital + physical copies available
- Certificates list competencies achieved

**Optional Exams:**
- Participants can take optional certification exams ($49-$99 each)
- Exams administered by WAI (online or in-person)
- Exams are separate from curriculum (additional fee)
