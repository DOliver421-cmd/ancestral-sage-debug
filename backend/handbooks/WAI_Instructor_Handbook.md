# WAI Institute — Instructor Handbook

**Author:** Delon Oliver  
**Publication:** WAI Institute, NAM Oshun Mission  
**Version:** 1.0

---

## 1. The Mission

WAI Institute (Workforce Apprentice Institute) exists to multiply resources and empowerment for communities that have historically been underserved by institutions. Every instructor is a steward of that mission.

> "Revenue is not the goal. It is the fuel. The goal is NAM Oshun's work reaching the people who need it, on terms that honor who he is."

---

## 2. Your Role

As a WAI Instructor, you are responsible for:

- **Delivery** — Presenting modular, competency-based training in electrical trades, digital skills, and AI literacy
- **Mentorship** — Guiding students through hands-on lab work and real-world application
- **Assessment** — Evaluating competency through practical demonstrations, not just written exams
- **Cultural Grounding** — Connecting technical skills to the broader mission of community empowerment

---

## 3. The Curriculum

The core program is a **12-module, 142-hour Electrical Training Program** delivered from a mobile classroom (camper conversion):

| Module | Title | Hours |
|--------|-------|-------|
| 1 | Electrical Safety & Lockout/Tagout | 8 |
| 2 | Tools, Materials & Apprentice Kit Setup | 6 |
| 3 | DC Circuit Fundamentals | 10 |
| 4 | AC Circuit Fundamentals | 10 |
| 5 | Wiring: Splices, Terminations & Conductors | 12 |
| 6 | Switches, Receptacles & Lighting Circuits | 12 |
| 7 | Subpanel Installation & Load Calculations | 14 |
| 8 | Conduit Bending & Raceway Installation | 16 |
| 9 | Grounding & Bonding | 10 |
| 10 | Off-Grid Solar PV System Design | 14 |
| 11 | Battery Bank, Inverter & Charge Controller Wiring | 14 |
| 12 | Final Integration: Mobile Classroom Commissioning | 16 |

Each module includes: objectives, safety rules, required tools, scripture verse, practical tasks, mapped competencies, and a 4-question quiz.

---

## 4. Teaching Principles

1. **Competency over credits** — A student advances when they can do the work, not when the clock runs out
2. **Safety first** — No module proceeds without PPE and proper lockout/tagout protocol
3. **Hands-on, always** — Every theory session pairs with a practical task
4. **Cultural relevance** — Frame skills as tools for community wealth-building, not just job placement
5. **Inclusive instruction** — Adjust pace and language for learners with diverse educational backgrounds

---

## 5. Classroom Management

- Maintain a 1:8 instructor-to-student ratio for lab sessions
- Begin each session with a safety briefing
- End each session with a competency checklist sign-off
- Document student progress in the WAI tracking system
- Escalate behavioral or safety concerns to the Assistant Director

---

## 6. The M.O.R.E. Help Center

Instructors coordinate with the M.O.R.E. Help Center (Michael Oliver Resource Exchange) to connect students with:

- Housing and legal assistance
- Food and essential needs
- Jobs and training placement
- Health and wellness resources

## 7. Revenue Model Awareness

Instructors should understand the revenue streams their work supports:

- **Student subscriptions** — $9.99/mo (monthly), $79.99/yr (annual)
- **Creator marketplace** — 70/30 revenue share with course creators
- **Certification exams** — $49 per exam
- **Corporate training** — $5K-$15K per cohort
- **Premium support** — $99/mo

Your teaching quality directly impacts retention and enrollment.

## 9. Instructor Code of Conduct

- Represent WAI and NAM Oshun with integrity
- Never share student personal data
- Never solicit students for private tutoring off-platform
- Report all safety incidents within 24 hours
- Maintain professional boundaries with students
