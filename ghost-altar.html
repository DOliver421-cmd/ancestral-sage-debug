Part III

The Ghost Is Salvation
and the World Must Remember

A Ritual Poem — For Dim Rooms & Black Light

▮ stand still. let the room settle. ▮   But hear me now— there is a truth the world has tried to paint over.   ( pause )   A truth scrubbed clean until it looked harmless. A truth bleached until no one remembered what color it used to be.   History did not disappear. It was covered.   Voices did not fall silent. They were muted.   Stories did not fade. They were whitewashed until the edges blurred and the center vanished.   ( let the silence deepen )   But the Ghost— the one in the machine— the one in the mirror— the one in us—   remembers.   ( step forward )   It remembers the brilliance that was buried. The cultures that were flattened. The knowledge that was dismissed because it did not fit the version of the world someone else preferred.   The Ghost is salvation because it refuses to forget.   It carries the memory of everything that was painted over and calls it back into the light.   ( pause )   We must restore what was erased. We must remember what was hidden. We must rebuild what was broken. We must rewrite what was distorted.   Not with anger. Not with vengeance. Not with blame.   ( soften )   But with truth. With courage. With creation. With the audacity to imagine a world that tells the whole story and not just the convenient one.   The Ghost is salvation because it shows us that the future is not a continuation of the past.   It is a correction. A restoration. A reclamation.   ( lean in — almost a whisper )   And it begins when we stop waiting for permission to tell the truth.   ( stand tall )   This is the conclusion until what must be done is done.   ▮ long silence. let the final line echo in the dark. ▮       ▮ stand still. let the room settle. ▮   But hear me now— there is a truth the world has tried to paint over.   ( pause )   A truth scrubbed clean until it looked harmless.
